## Module 1: Breathe and Anchor

**Time:** 5 minutes per day
**What it does:** Teaches your nervous system that you can regulate it. Not control it — regulate it. The difference matters.

### The One-Breath Reset
Breathe in for 4 counts. Hold for 2. Out for 6. That's it. The extended exhale activates your parasympathetic nervous system — the part that tells your body "you're safe right now." This isn't meditation. This is physiology.

Do three rounds. Morning. Midday. Before sleep. That's your anchor for the first week. Everything else in this kit builds on top of this.

### The 5-4-3-2-1 Ground
When things feel overwhelming and your mind is spinning:
- 5 things you can see right now
- 4 things you can hear
- 3 things you can feel (texture, temperature, pressure)
- 2 things you can smell
- 1 thing you can taste

This physically moves brain activity from the emotional centre back to the thinking centre. It takes 2 minutes and it works every time.

### Your Week 1 Practice
Morning: One-Breath Reset (3 rounds). That's all. If you do nothing else, do this.

## Module 2: The Fish Bowl

**Time:** 5 minutes when needed
**What it does:** Creates distance between you and your thoughts. You learn to watch your mind instead of being controlled by it.

### The Idea
Imagine your mind is a fish bowl. Your thoughts, feelings, urges, and fears are the fish swimming inside it. You are not the fish. You are the person watching the bowl.

When things get hard, most of us climb inside the bowl and become the fish — pulled around by every current, every craving, every fear. The Fish Bowl practice is simply the act of climbing back out. Watching. Noticing. Without judging what's swimming.

### The Practice
1. Notice a thought or urge arise. (A fish enters the bowl.)
2. Name it: "There's a craving." "There's fear." "There's shame." (You see the fish.)
3. Watch it without grabbing it. (Let it swim.)
4. If it doesn't serve you, let it pass. (It swims away.)
5. If it keeps coming back, that's okay. Name it again. It's just a persistent fish.

### The Shift
- Before: "I am my anxiety." (You ARE the fish.)
- After: "I notice anxiety is here." (You're watching the fish.)

That shift — from being the feeling to noticing the feeling — is the gap where choice lives. Right now that gap might be very small. That's okay. The practice widens it over time.

### Name It to Tame It
Research shows that naming an emotion reduces its intensity by about half. The act of putting a word to what you feel moves it from the emotional brain to the language brain — and gives you more control.

Instead of "I feel terrible" → "There's shame swimming in the bowl."
Instead of "I want to use" → "There's a craving. It's just a fish."
Instead of "I can't do this" → "There's fear here. I notice it."

## Module 3: Somatic Warnings — What Your Body Knows First

**Time:** 2 minutes, anytime
**What it does:** Teaches you to hear what your body is saying before your mind catches up. The body signals relapse hours or days before you consciously think about using.

### Your Body's Early Warning System
Before every relapse, the body sends signals. Jaw tightening. Gut churning. Chest heaviness. Shoulders creeping up. Hands clenching. Restlessness you can't explain. Fatigue that isn't about sleep.

These are not random. They're your nervous system telling you something is building. If you can learn to notice them, you can intervene before the thought even forms.

### The Body Check
Sit or stand still for 60 seconds. Scan from head to feet:
- Head: tension, pressure, fog?
- Jaw: clenched, tight, grinding?
- Shoulders and neck: raised, rigid, painful?
- Chest: heavy, tight, racing?
- Gut: churning, numb, hollow?
- Hands: clenched, restless, tingling?
- Legs: heavy, restless, wanting to move?

Whatever you find, name it. "There's tightness in my chest." That's all. You don't have to fix it. Just notice it. Noticing is the practice.

### Over Time
You'll start to recognise YOUR pattern. Maybe your jaw tightens two days before a craving hits. Maybe your gut churns when you're about to isolate. Maybe your hands get restless when The Bargain is swimming in the bowl. Your body has its own language. This module teaches you to read it.

## Module 4: Boundaries as Shoreline

**Time:** 10 minutes to read, ongoing to practise
**What it does:** Shows you where you end and someone else begins. Without boundaries, you drown in other people's water.

### The Basics
A boundary is not a wall. It's a shoreline. The ocean doesn't stop existing because the shore is there — the shore just defines where the land begins.

**You have the right to:**
- Say no without explaining why
- Change your mind
- Leave a conversation that's hurting you
- Not answer your phone
- Ask for what you need
- Take up space

**Boundary language:** "I choose to..." instead of "I have to..." "I'm not available for that right now." "I need to step away."

### The Energy Audit
At the end of any interaction, ask yourself: Do I feel drained or filled up? The answer tells you whether that interaction respected your shoreline or flooded it. You don't have to cut people off. You just need to notice which ones cost you energy you can't afford right now.

## Module 5: Micro-Rituals — The Daily Rhythm

**Time:** 3 minutes total per day
**What it does:** Gives your nervous system a predictable structure it can rely on. PAWS makes everything feel chaotic. Micro-rituals are the scaffolding that holds you steady while your brain heals.

### The Three Daily Hooks

**Morning (60 seconds):** One-Breath Reset (3 rounds) + one statement: "What's one thing I can do today?" Not five things. One.

**Midday (60 seconds):** Body check. Where's the tension? Name it. If Lonely is high, send the Connection Text. If Tired is high, rest without guilt.

**Evening (60 seconds):** "Today's fish." What was the most persistent thought today? Name it. Let it go. You don't have to solve it. Just notice it and release it. That's dopamine through letting go and serotonin through reflection.

### Why Rhythm Matters
Your brain is rebuilding its baseline. It needs repetition to form new pathways. These three hooks — morning, midday, evening — are not busy work. They're neurological scaffolding. Each one cycles at least one of the four chemicals your brain needs:
- Dopamine (progress, completion, "I did it")
- Oxytocin (connection, warmth, "I'm not alone")
- Serotonin (rhythm, recognition, "I matter")
- Endorphins (relief, joy, "I can breathe")

## Module 6: The Relapse Reframe

**Time:** 10 minutes after a setback
**What it does:** Replaces shame with learning. If a setback happens, this module turns it from a failure into information.

### The Truth About Setbacks
A setback is not the opposite of recovery. It is part of recovery. The question isn't "why did I fail?" The question is "what did the setback teach me?"

### Three Questions After a Setback
1. **What was the need?** What was the substance trying to provide? Calm? Numbness? Energy? Connection? Relief from pain? The craving was pointing at a real need. The substance was just the wrong solution.

2. **What did I learn?** What do I know about my pattern now that I didn't know before? Was there a Block I didn't recognise? A body signal I missed? A situation I wasn't prepared for?

3. **What's one thing different in the next 24 hours?** Not a life overhaul. One thing. One adjustment. One small change.

Write the answers down. You're extracting the gift from the fall. That's why it's called Gifts From The G.A.P.S. — not because the fall is a gift, but because there's a lesson buried in it that makes you harder to knock down next time.

**You are not back to zero. You are not starting over. You are starting from experience.**

## Module 7: The Return Protocol

**Time:** When you're ready to come back
**What it does:** Gives you a pathway to re-engage after stepping away — whether that's from this kit, from a program, from a worker, or from recovery itself.

### If You Stepped Away
That's okay. The Prodigal Son Protocol applies: you can return without penalty, without guilt, and without paperwork. Your seat was saved.

### Coming Back
1. **Re-anchor:** Module 1. One-Breath Reset. Three rounds. You remember this.
2. **Re-assess:** HALT check. Which one is it — Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired? Address the most urgent one first.
3. **Re-engage:** Pick one module to restart with. Just one. The one that feels most useful right now.
4. **Reach out if you're ready:** If you want to connect with a worker, call the number on your pocket card. If you don't have one, the crisis lines are always available. If you're not ready for that, keep working this kit. It's yours.
